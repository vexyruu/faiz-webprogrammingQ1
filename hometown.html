Surabaya

Surabaya, the capital of East Java province in Indonesia, is the country's second-largest city and a major trade and business hub. Known for its rich history, Surabaya played a significant role in Indonesia's struggle for independence. The city features a blend of modern and traditional architecture, with notable landmarks like the Heroes Monument and the iconic Suramadu Bridge connecting it to Madura Island. Surabaya is also famous for its vibrant culinary scene, offering a variety of local dishes, and is home to several cultural festivals. Its strategic location as a port city enhances its economic importance in the region.
